# telc B1 — section strategies & trap taxonomy

Grounded in current telc B1 guides plus the user's own recurring error patterns. Use for
`[discuss]`, `[retake]`, `[practice]`, and any "how do I do section X" / "why is this the
answer" question. Pair with `references/error-patterns-and-drills.md` for the user's
personal patterns.

---

## Leseverstehen

### Teil 1 (1–5) — headline/topic matching (global)
**How:** Read each short text for its **gist** (first + last sentence carry most of it),
then find the heading whose *meaning* matches — not the one sharing a word.
**Traps:**
- **Keyword-echo trap.** A heading repeats a word from the text but describes a different
  point. Match meaning, not vocabulary overlap.
- **Too-broad / too-narrow heading.** One heading covers only a detail in the text, another
  is broader than the text supports. The right one covers the text's *main* point.
- Extra distractor headings exist — don't force every heading to be used.

### Teil 2 (6–10) — multiple choice on one text (detail)
**How:** Read the question stem first, find the matching spot in the text, then test a/b/c
against exactly what the text says. Eliminating one option lifts your odds from 33%→50%.
**Traps:**
- **Comparison-direction trap.** "X is cheaper/better than Y" vs. the text saying the
  reverse. Check *which* thing the text rates higher.
- **Main-point vs. side-detail trap.** An option states something true but minor; the
  question asks for the main point (or vice-versa). Match the *level* the stem asks for.
- **Half-true option.** True in one clause, false in another → the whole option is wrong.

### Teil 3 (11–20) — situations → ads (a–l) + x (selective)
**How:** For each situation, list its **hard requirements** (place, time, activity, who).
Scan the ads for one that satisfies **all** of them. Learn classified abbreviations
(EG = Erdgeschoss, NK = Nebenkosten, VB = Verhandlungsbasis).
**Traps:**
- **X-overuse (the single most damaging error for this user).** Default to *finding a match
  first*. Only mark **x** after you've checked the ads and none satisfies every requirement.
  Marking x on a situation that had a match is a pure lost point. When in doubt between an
  imperfect ad and x, re-read the ad — telc ads are terse and the match is often there.
- **Partial-match trap.** An ad meets 2 of 3 requirements — that's not a match. All hard
  requirements must be met.
- **Twin-ad trap.** Two ads look similar; one fails on a small detail (date, region, price).

## Sprachbausteine

### Teil 1 (21–30) — MC cloze a/b/c (grammar)
**How:** For each gap, identify **what is being tested** (preposition+case? connector? verb
form? article? pronoun? reflexive?), then check gender/case/number/tense/word-order of each
option. Read the whole sentence — the trigger is often *before or after* the gap.
**High-frequency test points (and this user's recurring misses):**
- **Preposition selection + case** (biggest single SB topic). Learn verb+preposition and
  noun+preposition as fixed chunks (`warten auf`, `sich freuen auf/über`, `Angst vor`).
- **Reflexive verbs & their prepositions**; reflexive-verb **Perfekt** form (`hat sich …`
  vs. a wrong `wurde`).
- **Connector choice + the word order it forces** (see `connectors-bank.md`).
- **Singular/plural** and article agreement.

### Teil 2 (31–40) — word-bank cloze (vocabulary in context)
**How:** 15 words (a–o) for 10 gaps → **5 are decoys**. Eliminate as you place each word.
Teil 2 texts are almost always **letters/emails**, so the standard formal phrases
(`im Anhang finden Sie…`, `über eine Antwort würde ich mich freuen`, request/closing
formulas) fill 3–4 gaps almost for free.
**Traps (this user's weakest written section — SB T2):**
- **Connector fit.** The gap needs the connector matching the logical relation (contrast?
  cause? sequence?). Get the *logic* right first, then confirm the word order works.
- **Collocation fit.** Fixed phrases (`Bescheid geben`, `in Frage kommen`, `zur Verfügung
  stehen`) — the correct word is the one that completes the set expression.
- **Verb-form decoy.** Two candidate verbs fit the meaning; only one fits the grammar
  (subject agreement / tense) of the sentence.
**Fastest wins:** memorise the email/letter stock phrases and the top collocations — they
recur across exams and turn SB T2 from a weak spot into easy points.

## Hörverstehen — all richtig(+)/falsch(–)

The universal rule: **decide only after the statement's clause is complete in the audio.**
Most losses here are *comprehension-of-the-twist*, not vocabulary.

### The Lesepause (before each part)
Read every statement; underline the tested element (person / time / place / number /
opinion); flag negations and qualifiers; for Teil 2, infer the conversation's topic from
the 10 statements so you have a mental map.

### Teil 1 (41–45) — heard ONCE
Stay locked in; get the overall message; **wait for the end** — many short texts follow a
**setup → twist** shape ("Samstag kann ich leider nicht … aber die Schicht wurde abgesagt,
jetzt kann ich doch"). Deciding on the first clause gives the wrong sign.

### Teil 2 (46–55) — heard TWICE
First listen: answer the confident ones, dot the uncertain ones. Second listen: verify the
dots, watch qualifiers/negations. **Track who says what** — both speakers give opinions.

### Teil 3 (56–60) — heard TWICE
Pre-identify the one detail each statement targets; listen for *that*; let the rest wash
over. Second listen confirms.

### The +/− trap taxonomy (this user loses the most points here)
1. **Setup→twist reversal** (the recurring **Q51-type** error across Jan/Eva/Petra).
   Meaning flips after `aber / doch / trotzdem / eigentlich`. → wait for the whole clause.
2. **Negation missed.** `nicht, kein, nie, nichts, nicht mehr, noch nicht, erst, nur` — e.g.
   `erst am Freitag` = *not until* Friday, not "on Friday = yes".
3. **Similar numbers/times.** `dreizehn`(13) vs. `dreißig`(30), `vierzehn`(14) vs.
   `vierzig`(40). Listen for the **stressed syllable** (drei-ZEHN vs. DREI-ßig).
4. **Who-said-what** (Teil 2). Make sure the *named person* held that view, not the partner.
5. **Partial truth = falsch.** "Samstag **und** Sonntag" when only Saturday was said → –.
6. **Word-repetition, different context.** Same word in the audio, different reference
   (last Saturday vs. next Saturday) → the match is fake.
7. **Qualifier swap.** `alle` ≠ `die meisten`, `immer` ≠ `oft`, `beide` ≠ `einer` → –.
8. **Answer-sheet misalignment.** Transfer after **each part**; check task number 41→41. A
   one-row slip ruins everything after it.
9. **Blanks.** Never. 50/50 beats 0.

## Cross-section time & guessing rules
- No penalty for wrong answers on any objective section → **fill every item**.
- Get stuck → best guess, move on, come back if time remains.
- Protect the 30-minute letter slot inside the self-timed block; reading overrun is the
  usual thief.
