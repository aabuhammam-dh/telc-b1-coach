# Grammar for the telc B1 Sprachbausteine (high-frequency only)

Grounded in current telc B1 grammar guides. Grammar isn't tested in isolation — it runs
through Sprachbausteine (directly), reading (understanding structures), and writing
(producing them). This file is the **in-scope list** for the exam. For any single topic
the user wants explained, **web-search a reputable source first** (see the rule at the
bottom) and teach it simply and grounded — don't rely on memory alone.

Teaching format for any grammar point: **rule in plain English → 3 German examples with
glosses → the common mistake → the exam shortcut → a 3–5 item mini-drill.**

---

## The high-yield topics (study these; they cover most SB items)

### 1. Prepositions + correct case — *the single highest-yield SB topic*
- **Accusative:** durch, für, gegen, ohne, um.
- **Dative:** aus, bei, mit, nach, seit, von, zu.
- **Two-way (Wechselpräpositionen):** an, auf, hinter, in, neben, über, unter, vor,
  zwischen — **accusative for movement (wohin?)**, **dative for location (wo?)**.
  - `Ich gehe **in die** Schule.` (movement, acc) vs. `Ich bin **in der** Schule.` (location, dat)
- **Exam shortcut:** learn preposition + case as one chunk, and learn **verb+preposition**
  pairs as fixed units (see reflexive verbs). SB tests these directly and often.

### 2. Connectors + the word order they force — *extremely high-yield*
Three classes (full list + examples in `connectors-bank.md`):
- **Coordinating (no word-order change):** und, aber, oder, denn, sondern.
- **Subordinating (verb to the END):** weil, dass, wenn, obwohl, als, bevor, nachdem,
  damit, ob.
- **Adverbial connectors (inversion — verb before subject):** deshalb, deswegen, darum,
  trotzdem, außerdem, dann, danach, jedoch.
- **Exam shortcut:** first pick the connector whose **logic** fits (cause / contrast /
  sequence / purpose), then confirm the option's **word order** matches its class. SB
  frequently offers weil / deshalb / obwohl / trotzdem as the four choices for one gap.

### 3. Reflexive verbs (+ their fixed preposition)
- `sich freuen auf/über`, `sich interessieren für`, `sich bewerben um`, `sich ärgern über`,
  `sich kümmern um`, `sich treffen mit`.
- `Ich freue **mich auf** den Urlaub.` · `Er interessiert **sich für** Musik.`
- **Common mistake (this user):** wrong reflexive Perfekt — it's `hat **sich** gefreut`,
  not a passive `wurde`. And keep the reflexive pronoun (`freue **mich**`).

### 4. Konjunktiv II — polite forms (also vital for writing & speaking)
- `haben → hätte` (`Ich hätte gern …`), `sein → wäre` (`Es wäre schön, wenn …`),
  `können → könnte` (`Könnten Sie bitte …?`), `werden → würde` (`Ich würde gern …`),
  `sollen → sollte` (`Du solltest …`).
- **Exam shortcut:** when unsure of a verb's Konjunktiv II, use **würde + infinitive**.

### 5. Verb tenses in scope
- **Präsens** (now / fixed future with a time word), **Perfekt** (standard spoken/informal
  past: `haben/sein` + Partizip II), **Präteritum** (formal writing + always for `sein,
  haben, modal verbs`: war, hatte, konnte, wollte…), **Futur I** (`werden` + infinitive).
- **`sein` in Perfekt** for: movement A→B (gehen, fahren, kommen), change of state
  (einschlafen, aufwachen), and sein/werden/bleiben. Otherwise `haben` (default when unsure).

### 6. Relative clauses / relative pronouns
- Pronoun agrees in **gender + number** with its noun; its **case** comes from its job in
  the relative clause; verb goes to the end.
  - Nom: `Der Mann, **der** dort steht, …` · Akk: `Das Buch, **das** ich lese, …` ·
    Dat: `Die Frau, **der** ich geholfen habe, …`

### 7. Verb position (the backbone; central-error territory in writing)
- Main clause: finite verb is **2nd**. Subordinate clause (weil/dass/wenn…): finite verb is
  **last**. After an adverbial connector or a fronted element: **inversion** (verb before
  subject). `Deshalb **bleibe ich** zu Hause.`

### 8. Lighter-touch topics (recognise; don't over-drill)
- **Passiv:** `werden` + Partizip II (`Das Haus **wird** renoviert.`; past `**wurde**
  renoviert`; perfect `ist renoviert **worden**`). Appears in reading; recognise it.
- **Adjective endings:** know the common pattern — if the article already shows
  gender/case, the ending is weak (-e/-en); if not, the ending carries it (strong). Don't
  sink exam prep into the full table.

---

## Out of scope for B1 (do NOT spend prep time here)
Genitiv beyond the most common set phrases; Plusquamperfekt; Futur II; full passive
paradigms; Konjunktiv I; complex nominal-style constructions; exhaustive adjective-ending
tables. Recognising them in a reading text is fine — producing them is not required.

---

## The search-first rule for grammar explanations (firm)
When the user asks you to explain any grammar point or wants examples:
1. **Web-search a reputable German-grammar source first** and ground the explanation in it
   — e.g. `mein-deutschbuch.de`, `easy-deutsch.de`, `deutsch-lernen.com`, `germanup.org`,
   `dw.com`, `lingolia.com`. Prefer teaching/official sites over forums.
2. Then teach in the format above, keep it **simple**, and **cite** what you grounded on.
3. Tie it to the telc task where it shows up; skip out-of-scope theory.
The user explicitly wants easy explanations that are **grounded on real sources**, not
recalled from memory.
