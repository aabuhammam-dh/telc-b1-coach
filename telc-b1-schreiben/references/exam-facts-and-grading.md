# telc B1 Schriftlicher Ausdruck — structure, rubric & scoring (authoritative)

Source of truth: telc's own *Zertifikat Deutsch (telc Deutsch B1) Übungstest 1*,
scoring pages 41–43. Everything here matches that document.

## 1. Where the letter sits in the exam

| Part (written) | Items | Points | Weight |
|---|---|---|---|
| Leseverstehen | 20 | 75 | 25% |
| Sprachbausteine | 20 | 30 | 10% |
| Hörverstehen | 20 | 75 | 25% |
| **Schriftlicher Ausdruck (Brief)** | 1 letter | **45** | **15%** |
| **Written total** | | **225** | |
| Oral total | | 75 | 25% |
| **Exam total** | | **300** | 100% |

- **Time for the letter: 30 minutes.** It is the last thing in the written exam,
  written on a separate answer sheet, by hand.
- **Pass = 60% of each half:** ≥135/225 written **and** ≥45/75 oral. There is no
  official standalone pass mark for the letter — but a letter scoring 0 (zero rule)
  or a weak band makes the 135 written target much harder, because it's 45 of 225.

## 2. The task, always the same shape

1. A short **scenario** (who you're writing to and why) — this decides register.
2. The **letter/email you received**, ~4–6 lines, signed by a name.
3. `Antworten Sie …` + **4 Leitpunkte** (guiding points) as bullets.
4. An **instruction line** telling you to pick a sensible order and include the
   required framing elements. Which elements vary by version:
   - Classic letter version: *"Vergessen Sie Datum und Anrede nicht"* → include
     **Datum + Anrede + Einleitung + Schluss**.
   - Email version: *"eine passende Betreff, eine passende Anrede …"* → include
     **Betreff + Anrede + Einleitung + Schluss** (no date needed).
   Always follow whatever this line names.

**Register is not a choice.** "an die Firma / an das Hotel / an die Behörde / Sie"
→ formal (`Sehr geehrte …`). "dein Freund / liebe(r) … / du" → informal (`Liebe/r …`).

## 3. The grading rubric — verbatim descriptors

Two graders score independently and agree on one grade. Three criteria, each A/B/C/D.

### Kriterium I — Berücksichtigung der Leitpunkte
> A (5): Alle vier vorgegebenen Leitpunkte werden inhaltlich angemessen bearbeitet
> B (3): Drei Leitpunkte werden inhaltlich angemessen bearbeitet
> C (1): Zwei Leitpunkte werden inhaltlich angemessen bearbeitet
> D (0): Nur einer oder keiner … wird inhaltlich angemessen bearbeitet

"Inhaltlich angemessen bearbeitet" = you say something **relevant and developed**
about the point — roughly a real sentence that engages it, not a bare mention.

### Kriterium II — Kommunikative Gestaltung
Judges four things:
> 1. die sinnvolle Anordnung der Leitpunkte
> 2. die Verknüpfung der Sätze/Äußerungseinheiten
> 3. die inhalts- und adressatenbezogene Ausdrucksweise (incl. correct register)
> 4. der Adressatenbezug (Datum, Anrede, Gruß-/Abschiedsformel)

> A (5): voll angemessen · B (3): im Großen und Ganzen angemessen ·
> C (1): kaum noch akzeptabel · D (0): insgesamt nicht ausreichend

### Kriterium III — Formale Richtigkeit (Syntax, Morphologie, Orthografie)
> A (5): keine oder nur vereinzelte Fehler
> B (3): Fehler, die das Verständnis nicht beeinträchtigen
> C (1): Fehler an zentralen Stellen, die das Verständnis erheblich beeinträchtigen
> D (0): so viele Fehler, dass der Text kaum noch verständlich ist

### Zusatzpunkte (IV) — up to +2
> +1 for above-average **sprachliche Vielfalt** (Wortschatz, Strukturen)
> +1 for above-average **Umfang** (inhaltliche Gestaltung)
> Not given if (a) the letter already scored full marks, or (b) any of the three
> criteria is C or worse.

### THE ZERO RULE (verbatim)
> Wenn Kriterium I und/oder Kriterium III mit „D" bewertet wurde, wird der gesamte
> Brief mit 0 Punkten bewertet.

→ Address 0–1 of the 4 points, **or** write something barely comprehensible, and the
letter is 0 regardless of the rest. Kriterium II being D does **not** by itself zero
the letter — only I and/or III do.

## 4. Turning A/B/C/D into the /45

1. Points: A=5, B=3, C=1, D=0 for each of I, II, III. Sum = raw, max **15**.
2. Bonus: +0/+1/+2, but only if eligible (no criterion C-or-worse; not already 15).
   Because bonus is blocked at full marks, the effective raw ceiling stays 15.
3. **Multiply the raw total by 3** → final, max **45**.

### Worked examples
- **Clean, all 4 points, well-linked, formal register correct, one tiny slip:**
  I=A(5), II=A(5), III=A(5) → 15 raw → **45/45**. (No bonus — already full.)
- **All 4 points, good structure, several case-after-preposition errors that don't
  block meaning:** I=A(5), II=A(5), III=B(3) → 13 raw, eligible for bonus →
  +2 → capped 15 → **45/45**, or +1 → 14 → **42/45** depending on variety/scope.
- **Only 3 points developed (4th too thin), otherwise fine:** I=B(3), II=B(3),
  III=B(3) → 9 raw → **27/45** — a solid pass, but the missed point cost ~6 marks.
- **Only 2 points, messy links, many central errors:** I=C(1), II=C(1), III=C(1)
  → 3 raw → **9/45** — failing; no bonus (all C).
- **Wrote a lot but only touched 1 point:** I=D → **ZERO** regardless.

## 5. What this means strategically (drill into the user)

- **Never lose a whole Leitpunkt.** Going from 4→3 points addressed is I: A→B, a
  6-mark swing (5→3, ×3). Going to only 1 point is the zero rule. Covering all four,
  even briefly, is the highest-value move in the whole task.
- **Keep it comprehensible above all** — Kriterium III D also zeros the letter.
  Simple correct sentences protect you here.
- **Case errors after prepositions are "tolerable"** (Kriterium III B territory —
  "Fehler, die das Verständnis nicht beeinträchtigen"). They cost you the A on III,
  not the pass. Verb-position errors are "central" and hurt much more.
- **Register is Kriterium II.** A formal letter written with "du", or an informal
  one with "Sehr geehrte", damages II even if grammar is fine.
- **The bonus is real but small.** Don't chase fancy vocabulary at the cost of
  correctness — a C anywhere kills the bonus anyway.

## 6. 30-minute exam plan (teach this timing)

- **0–4 min — read & plan.** Identify register, underline the 4 Leitpunkte, jot 1–2
  keyword ideas per point, pick an order. (Order of points needn't match the task.)
- **4–24 min — write.** One sentence per line for control. 2–3 sentences per point.
  Datum/Betreff + Anrede + short Einleitung + the four points linked with connectors
  + a Schluss + closing formula.
- **24–30 min — two check passes, one thing at a time.**
  - Pass 1: **verb position** in every sentence (finite verb 2nd in main clauses;
    at the end in weil/dass/wenn clauses).
  - Pass 2: **dative/accusative** after prepositions and check every Leitpunkt is
    actually there.
  Don't check everything at once — one target per pass finds more.
