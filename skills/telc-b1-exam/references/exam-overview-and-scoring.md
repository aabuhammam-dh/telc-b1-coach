# telc Deutsch B1 — structure, scoring & exam-day (authoritative)

Grounded in telc's own materials and current (2026) exam guides. This is **telc Deutsch
B1**, the general adult exam — *not* the DTZ, and *not* Goethe B1 (which is modular with a
different letter). If a source describes modules you can pass separately, or a "Brief +
Diskussionsbeitrag", it's describing a different exam.

## 1. The whole exam

| Part | Skill | Items | Points | Weight | Timing |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Leseverstehen | reading | 20 | 75 | 25% | in the written block |
| Sprachbausteine | grammar/vocab | 20 | 30 | 10% | in the written block |
| Hörverstehen | listening | 20 | 75 | 25% | ~30 min (audio-paced) |
| Schriftlicher Ausdruck | writing (1 letter) | 1 | 45 | 15% | ~30 min |
| **Written total** | | | **225** | 75% | ~2.5 h |
| Mündliche Prüfung | speaking (pair) | — | 75 | 25% | ~15 min + ~20 min prep |
| **Exam total** | | | **300** | 100% | |

- **Reading + Sprachbausteine + writing** are one continuous written block (~90 min) —
  you manage your own time across them. **Listening** is separate and audio-paced (you
  can't pause/replay).
- The **oral** is a separate pair exam with two examiners, usually after a break.

## 2. Passing rule (verbatim intent)

> You must reach **at least 60% of each half separately**: **≥135/225 written** AND
> **≥45/75 oral.** The two halves are **not** averaged. A near-perfect written result
> cannot rescue a failed oral, and vice-versa.

Many test centres let you **retake only the failed half** later — confirm with the centre.

## 3. Each section in detail

### Leseverstehen (20 items, 75 pts)
- **Teil 1 (1–5) — Global.** Match 5 short texts to headings/topics from a longer list
  (extra headings are distractors).
- **Teil 2 (6–10) — Detail.** One longer text; 5 multiple-choice items (a/b/c).
- **Teil 3 (11–20) — Selective.** 10 everyday situations, matched to short classified-style
  ads labelled **a–l**; if no ad fits a situation, mark **x**. (Some situations legitimately
  have no match — but see the X-overuse trap in `section-strategies.md`.)

### Sprachbausteine (20 items, 30 pts)
- **Teil 1 (21–30).** A text (often a letter/email) with 10 gaps; each gap = choose a/b/c.
  Tests **grammar**: prepositions + case, connectors, verb forms, articles, pronouns,
  reflexive/separable verbs, relative pronouns.
- **Teil 2 (31–40).** A text with 10 gaps and a **word bank a–o (15 words)** — choose the
  fitting word; **5 words are never used**. Tests **vocabulary in context**: connectors,
  fixed collocations, coherence.
- Worth only **30 points total** (1.5 pts/item) but the fastest points to win because it's
  pattern-based. Never leave a gap blank — no penalty for wrong answers.

### Hörverstehen (20 items, 75 pts) — all richtig(+)/falsch(–)
- **Teil 1 (41–45) — Global, heard ONCE.** 5 short separate texts (voicemails,
  announcements, short monologues). One R/F statement each. The single listen is the whole
  test for these — meaning often flips in the last sentence.
- **Teil 2 (46–55) — Detail, heard TWICE.** One longer conversation; 10 R/F statements.
  Most demanding part. Track **who says what**.
- **Teil 3 (56–60) — Selective, heard TWICE.** 5 short info-dense announcements
  (opening hours, prices, platforms, weather). One R/F statement each — hunt for one detail.
- Mark on the Antwortbogen with a soft pencil. **Never leave blank** — a guess is 50/50,
  a blank is 0.

### Schriftlicher Ausdruck (1 letter, 45 pts) → telc-b1-schreiben skill
- One reply-letter/email, 30 min, ~120–150 words, 4 Leitpunkte to cover. **This skill does
  not handle it** — activate `telc-b1-schreiben`.

### Mündliche Prüfung (75 pts) → `references/oral-exam.md`
- Pair exam, ~15 min + ~20 min prep (notes allowed, no dictionary). Three parts:
  Kontaktaufnahme · über ein Thema sprechen · gemeinsam etwas planen.

## 4. Raw-item score vs. the real weighted pass line

The project logs raw items (**x/60**, with 36/60 = 60% as a rough "in the zone" marker).
That proxy is fine for tracking trends, but the **real** pass is computed on weighted
points, and the 45-point letter is inside the 225. Convert like this:

- Leseverstehen: each of 20 items ≈ **3.75 pts** → x/20 items = (x × 3.75)/75.
- Hörverstehen: same, **3.75 pts/item**.
- Sprachbausteine: each of 20 items = **1.5 pts** → x/20 items = (x × 1.5)/30.
- Objective sections together = **180 pts**; the letter adds **45** → **225**.

**What "am I passing?" really needs.** Written pass = 135/225. So:
- With **0** on the letter, you'd need **135/180 objective = 75%** of objective points.
- With a **solid letter (~30/45)**, you need **105/180 objective ≈ 58%**.
- With a **strong letter (~40/45)**, you need **95/180 objective ≈ 53%**.

→ Report the raw x/60 for tracking, but when the user asks whether they'll pass, reason in
weighted points and stress that the **letter is 45 points of the 225** — neglecting it is
the most common way a strong reader/listener still fails the written half. Point them to
the `telc-b1-schreiben` skill to secure those 45.

## 5. Exam-day procedure (tell the user)

- Arrive ~30 min early; bring valid photo ID (no ID = no entry). Phones/materials away.
- **Written block first** (~90 min: reading, Sprachbausteine, writing — self-timed), then
  **listening** (~30 min, audio-paced).
- Break, then the **oral**: ~20 min prep (make notes, no dictionary), then ~15 min exam.
- Certificate usually arrives a few weeks later.

## 6. Time budget for the self-timed written block (~90 min)
- Leseverstehen ≈ **15 / 20 / 25 min** across T1 / T2 / T3 (T3 is the time sink).
- Sprachbausteine ≈ **15–20 min** total (most items <1 min if you know the pattern).
- Schriftlicher Ausdruck = **30 min** — protect this; it's 45 points. Don't let reading
  overrun eat the letter.
- If stuck on any item: mark a best guess and move on. No penalty for wrong answers
  anywhere on the objective sections.
