# Errors that cost points vs. errors you can live with (telc B1)

The rubric rewards **comprehensible, complete, correctly-framed** letters. Not all
mistakes are equal. Teach the user to spend their limited attention on the ones that
actually move a grade.

---

## The one distinction that matters: central vs. tolerable errors

Kriterium III separates **"Fehler an zentralen Stellen, die das Verständnis erheblich
beeinträchtigen"** (→ C, hurts a lot) from **"Fehler, die das Verständnis nicht
beeinträchtigen"** (→ still B, survivable).

### CENTRAL errors — hunt these down, they drop you to C and risk the zero rule
- **Verb in the wrong position** (see below). This is the #1 killer of B1 letters.
- **A wrong word that changes or hides the meaning** (e.g. wrong verb, a noun that
  doesn't fit).
- **A broken subordinate clause** where the reader loses the thread.
- **Missing/garbled negation** ("nicht"/"kein") that flips the meaning.
- **Leaving out a Leitpunkt** — not a grammar error, but the biggest single loss
  (Kriterium I) and the zero-rule trigger.

### TOLERABLE errors — don't panic, fix if time allows, but they won't sink you
- **Case ending after a preposition** when the meaning is still clear:
  *mit dem Zug* written as *mit den Zug*, *auf dem Boden* as *auf den Boden*,
  *wegen meiner Arbeit* as *wegen mein Arbeit*. The course's own "gut"-graded sample
  letters contain exactly these — they cap you at **B on Kriterium III**, they do
  **not** fail you. Meaning survives, so it's tolerable.
- **A wrong adjective ending** (*ein neue Job* for *einen neuen Job*).
- **A minor spelling slip** that's still readable.
- **Missing comma before a subordinate clause.**

> Takeaway for the user: **Get the verb in the right place and cover all four points.**
> Then, if there's time, tidy the der/dem/den after prepositions. Chasing perfect case
> endings while a verb sits in the wrong slot is optimising the wrong thing.

## Verb position — the rules that protect Kriterium III

**Rule 1 — Main clause: the finite verb is in position 2.**
Whatever you put first, the verb stays second and the subject moves after it.
- `Ich fahre morgen nach Berlin.`
- Start with time → verb still 2nd: `Morgen **fahre** ich nach Berlin.`
  (Common mistake: *Morgen ich fahre …* ❌)
- After `deshalb`, `trotzdem`, `dann`: verb 2nd → *Deshalb **komme** ich später.*

**Rule 2 — weil / dass / wenn / obwohl: finite verb goes to the END.**
- `Ich bleibe zu Hause, weil ich krank **bin**.`
- `Ich hoffe, dass du bald **kommst**.`
  (Common mistake: *…, weil ich bin krank* ❌)

**Rule 3 — denn keeps normal order (unlike weil).**
- `Ich komme nicht, denn ich **habe** keine Zeit.` (verb 2nd, no inversion)

**Rule 4 — Modal/Perfekt: the second verb part goes to the end.**
- `Ich möchte dich bald **besuchen**.`
- `Ich habe drei Nächte im Hotel **verbracht**.`

Two-minute drill you can give the user: hand them five short sentences with the verb
misplaced and have them fix only the verb position.

## Register traps (Kriterium II)
- **du vs. Sie must match the scenario and stay consistent.** Formal → `Sie`, `Ihnen`,
  `Ihr/Ihre`; informal → `du`, `dir`, `dein/deine`. Slipping between them mid-letter
  is a classic Kriterium II hit.
- **Wrong salutation for the register:** `Sehr geehrte` in a letter to a friend, or
  `Hallo` to a company. Match salutation ↔ closing (`Sehr geehrte…` ↔
  `Mit freundlichen Grüßen`; `Liebe…` ↔ `Liebe/Viele Grüße`).
- **After the salutation comma, next word is lowercase** (unless noun/"Sie").
  `Liebe Anna,` → `vielen Dank …` not `Vielen Dank …`.
- **`Mit freundlichen Grüßen` has no comma after it.**

## Structure traps (Kriterium II)
- **Stub sentences with no connectors** read as a list, not a letter. Link ideas with
  weil / aber / deshalb / außerdem.
- **No greeting/closing, or missing required Betreff/Datum** — free marks lost.
- **Answering points in a random, jumpy order.** A sensible order (often mirroring the
  received letter's questions) is explicitly rewarded.

## Content traps (Kriterium I — the expensive ones)
- **Only mentioning a point in passing** ("Musik mag ich auch") without developing it
  may not count as "inhaltlich angemessen bearbeitet." Give each point ~2 sentences.
- **Misreading a Leitpunkt** and writing about the wrong thing = that point not
  addressed. Underline each point before writing.
- **Running out of time and dropping the 4th point.** Budget so every point gets
  something — a thin fourth point still beats a missing one (B vs. worse).

## The 6-point pre-submit checklist (the user runs this in the last minutes)
1. **All four Leitpunkte present** and each actually developed? (protects I + zero rule)
2. **Register consistent** (du/Sie), correct salutation **and** matching closing? (II)
3. **Betreff/Datum** included **if** the task asked for them? (II)
4. **Verb position** correct in every sentence — 2nd in main, end in weil/dass/wenn? (III)
5. **Reads clearly** start to finish — no sentence where meaning is lost? (protects III
   + zero rule)
6. **der/dem/den after prepositions** tidied where quick — last, lowest priority. (III)
